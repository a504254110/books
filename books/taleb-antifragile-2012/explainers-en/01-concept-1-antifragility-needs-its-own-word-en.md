## Core idea

Taleb says ordinary language is missing an important category. A thing can be fragile, robust, or antifragile.

## Detailed explanation

The example separates `robust` from `antifragile`. A robust package can survive rough handling. An antifragile package benefits from rough handling.

A common mistake is to think Taleb is only renaming resilience. His actual point is that resilience returns to baseline, while antifragility improves through stress.

## What this example is really showing

A normal package marked `fragile` tells people to handle it carefully because shaking or dropping it will damage what is inside. Taleb then asks the reader to imagine the real opposite kind of package: one that improves when it is shaken or dropped.
