## Core idea

When the system is not well understood and the downside of intervention is hidden, waiting is often superior to action. Delay is not always weakness; it can be disciplined restraint. 

## Detailed explanation

These cases show why delay can be a filter. By waiting, the decision-maker gives the system a chance to heal itself or reveal whether action is truly needed. 

Taleb is reversing the prestige hierarchy of modern professionalism, where visible action gets rewarded and avoided harm stays invisible. In the whole book, this anticipates Taleb's via negativa strategy: subtractive action is often more rigorous than additive action. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb praises the doctor who avoids unnecessary back surgery and also points to Fabius Cunctator, who drove Hannibal mad by refusing rushed engagement. He adds his own case of postponing spinal surgery and recovering after hiking and weight training instead.
