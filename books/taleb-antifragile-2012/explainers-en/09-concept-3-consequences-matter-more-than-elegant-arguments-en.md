## Core idea

In real life, the best heuristic is not "Who sounds right?" but "Who is exposed to being wrong?" Fat Tony's strength comes from tracking consequences rather than debate performance. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because Taleb's rule is not "never predict." It is "watch who becomes fragile by pretending to predict." The profitable move is often to stand on the other side of overconfidence. 

This chapter begins moving from social portrait to decision rule. It is the first practical expression of a nonpredictive worldview. In the whole book, this logic develops in chapter 23 into the full doctrine of skin in the game. 

## What this example is really showing

Fat Tony does not believe in prediction in the grand expert sense. Instead, he bets that the people who rely on prediction and ignore fragility will eventually blow up. Taleb also describes Nero opening the account statement from the "Fat Tony bet" as a reminder that real action, not praise, is the final test.
