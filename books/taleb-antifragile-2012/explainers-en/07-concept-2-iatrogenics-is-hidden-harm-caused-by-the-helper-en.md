## Core idea

Harm generated by intervention is often delayed, diffuse, or hard to attribute, which is why helpers systematically underestimate it.

## Detailed explanation

These cases show why iatrogenics stays hidden for so long. The healer is socially coded as a helper, so the damage from treatment is harder to see and slower to admit.

Taleb needs a concept that captures not just failure to help, but damage produced by the attempt to help. In the whole book, the whole of Book VI later develops this idea into a set of heuristics for medicine and lifestyle.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses George Washington's death after heavy bloodletting and Semmelweis's discovery that hospitals were killing women through hospital fever. In both cases, treatment and medical institutions were adding harm while appearing to provide care.
