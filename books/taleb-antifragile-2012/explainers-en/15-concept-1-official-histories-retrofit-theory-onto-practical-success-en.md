## Core idea

Many historical accounts are written from the perspective of later interpreters who attribute success to the kinds of knowledge they personally value. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because it shows the mechanism of expropriation directly. Once practice works, the cleaner written account often overwrites the actual order of events. 

Taleb is exposing the narrative laundering that protects academic prestige while downgrading practitioners. In the whole book, this prepares the later ethical critique of professions that profit from distortions without paying for them. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb starts with derivatives traders using complex option payoffs successfully before the academic formulas were derived. He and Espen Haug then trace how academics later packaged those formulas as if traders had been using theory first. Taleb even describes an editor trying to rewrite his firsthand account so the academic arrow would point the preferred way.
