## Core idea

Antifragility is often a property of the collective, not each individual unit. Some parts must be allowed to fail so the whole can adapt. 

## Detailed explanation

These scenes show why Taleb keeps talking about layers. The failure hurts the individual business, but the wider system gains because many small experiments are testing the environment at the same time. 

This keeps the reader from interpreting antifragility as a guarantee of personal survival. Taleb's framework often works at the level of populations, not individuals. In the whole book, this layered view becomes crucial later in Taleb's ethical argument about who is allowed to fail and who gets protected. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses startups and restaurants. A single startup or restaurant is fragile and often fails, but that fragility lets many experiments run at once. Better ideas survive, weak ones disappear, and the wider economy gets variety instead of one frozen menu forever.
