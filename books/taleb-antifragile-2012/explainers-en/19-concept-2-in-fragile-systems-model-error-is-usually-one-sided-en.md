## Core idea

Some mistakes wash out over time, but mistakes in fragile systems do not. They mostly add harm. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because it separates neutral error from fragile error. In a concave system, the same size mistake hurts more than it helps, so the average effect of error is negative. 

This is why Taleb keeps warning against neat models in economics and medicine. When harm is one-sided, a model that looks slightly wrong on paper can be deadly in practice. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb contrasts two cases. In trading, buying two thousand units by mistake instead of one thousand can help or hurt, and many such errors can cancel out over time. But in traffic, wars, or delayed flights, disturbances mostly make things worse, not better.
