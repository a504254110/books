## Core idea

Medical benefit often rises sharply with severity, while iatrogenic risk stays present. That is why aggressive treatment makes more sense for the very sick than for near-healthy people.

## Detailed explanation

The same intervention changes meaning across cases. A drug or procedure can be sensible near ruin and still be a sucker's trade for someone only slightly outside the normal range.

This is not a call to ignore early warning signs. It is a call to distinguish robust lifestyle changes from medication or procedures with hidden downside.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses blood-pressure treatment probabilities to make the point concrete. In mild cases only about one in eighteen benefits, but in severe cases the benefit rises much more sharply. He then contrasts this with surgery for trauma, where nature clearly cannot do the job alone.
