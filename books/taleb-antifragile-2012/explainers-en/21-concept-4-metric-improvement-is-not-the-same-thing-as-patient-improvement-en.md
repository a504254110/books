## Core idea

Lowering a clinical marker is not automatically the same thing as making the patient healthier.

## Detailed explanation

Medicine can become a measurement game. A number improves on paper while the patient absorbs costs that are delayed, diffuse, or legally invisible.

This is another version of Taleb's `not the same thing` warning. It also anticipates chapter 22, where he argues that health often improves more through subtraction than through metric-driven addition.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses statins and cholesterol to show the trap. The drug can improve the tracked number, but for many lower-risk people the real benefit is weak while hidden harms remain uncertain. He compares this to muzzling a baby to stop crying without fixing the cause.
