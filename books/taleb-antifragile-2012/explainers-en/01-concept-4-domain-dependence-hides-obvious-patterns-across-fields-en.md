## Core idea

People often understand beneficial stress in one area, then miss the same pattern in another area. 

## Detailed explanation

The issue is not total ignorance. The same person can understand stress training in one setting and reject the same logic in another. Taleb uses that gap to explain why antifragility often looks obvious only after someone points to the domain mismatch. 

This explains Taleb's method in the rest of the book. He keeps jumping across biology, medicine, politics, and finance because he believes the same structure keeps appearing. 

A common confusion is this: A repeated pattern across fields does not automatically mean the same mechanism is operating in each field. This is where some readers will follow Taleb and others will resist him. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb sees a Dubai banker pay a porter to carry luggage, then later pay to lift heavy kettlebells in the gym as if he were carrying a suitcase. He also imagines a doctor who praises exercise because it makes the body tougher, then quickly prescribes antibiotics for a minor infection instead of letting the immune system handle it.
