## Core idea

A thing can be half-invented for a very long time. The real breakthrough is often implementation, not theoretical discovery.

## Detailed explanation

Obvious ideas are often only obvious in hindsight. Practice has to recognize the option and carry it into use before the invention changes life.

Taleb is extending optionality from finance into innovation. The same logic drives the next chapters on green lumber and rewritten history.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb starts with the wheeled suitcase: humanity had wheels for thousands of years, yet travelers still dragged heavy luggage by hand until someone put small wheels directly under the suitcase. He adds similar cases: Mesoamericans had toy wheels but not wheeled transport, the Greeks had an early steam engine as a curiosity, and Steve Jobs had to make the mouse and graphical interface usable on an ordinary desk.

The point of the scene: Obvious ideas are often only obvious in hindsight. Practice has to recognize the option and carry it into use before the invention changes life.
