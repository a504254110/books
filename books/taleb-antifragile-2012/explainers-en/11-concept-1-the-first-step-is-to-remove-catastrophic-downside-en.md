## Core idea

Antifragility begins with not being destroyed. Before optimizing for upside, one must first reduce exposure to terminal or irreversible harm.

## Detailed explanation

The sequence of events changes everything. Once ruin or irreversible harm happens, later good conditions do not fully repair the damage.

This is Taleb's operational correction to people who hear "gain from disorder" and immediately think of reckless risk taking. In the whole book, the emphasis on irreversible damage later returns in the nonlinear chapters, where Taleb formalizes why downside dominates.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb returns to the broken package from chapter 1 and adds other path-dependent scenes. A kidney-stone operation before anesthesia means the patient suffers the cutting while still conscious; anesthesia before surgery means protection comes first. He then gives a financial version: a university endowment can report years of strong returns, then lose everything in one bad year if the hidden fragility was large enough.

The point of the scene: The sequence of events changes everything. Once ruin or irreversible harm happens, later good conditions do not fully repair the damage.
