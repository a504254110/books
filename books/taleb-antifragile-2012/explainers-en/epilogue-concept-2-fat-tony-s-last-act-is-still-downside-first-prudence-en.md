## Core idea

Even after death, Fat Tony is presented as conservative where downside matters and trusting where explicit rulebooks would be too rigid. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because it condenses Tony's role in the book. He controls ruin where control is possible, but he still leaves room for judgment, optionality, and unscripted action. 

The estate scene ties back to Seneca, the barbell, and skin in the game. Tony's final trust in Nero also fits Taleb's preference for embodied judgment over bureaucratic instruction. 

A common confusion is this: The epilogue is more literary than analytic. That is deliberate. Its job is to leave the book in lived image, not to prove another theorem. 

## What this example is really showing

Nero discovers that Tony's estate is debt-free, conservative, and fairly distributed. Then comes the posthumous prank: Tony leaves Nero twenty million dollars for a noble but secret and dangerous mission, trusting Nero to read his mind.
