## Core idea

Theoretical articulation frequently follows practical problem solving rather than causing it. What works becomes legible after the fact.

## Detailed explanation

It breaks the default school story. In many domains, practice is not the last step of theory. It is the source from which later theory is abstracted.

This is Taleb's most direct challenge to the linear model that runs from science to technology to wealth. In the whole book, this reinforces the optionality and tinkering logic from chapters 12 and 13.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb stacks examples from several fields: jet engines were built through trial and adjustment before theory could fully explain them, cybernetics was in practice before Wiener was credited with founding it, and medieval builders raised cathedrals and Roman engineers built aqueducts with heuristics long before formal mathematics became central.

The point of the scene: It breaks the default school story. In many domains, practice is not the last step of theory. It is the source from which later theory is abstracted.
