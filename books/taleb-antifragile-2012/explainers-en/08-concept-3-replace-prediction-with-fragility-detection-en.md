## Core idea

When events are not forecastable, competence should be judged by whether one can identify who is vulnerable, who is robust, and who benefits from disorder.

## Detailed explanation

These examples show what Taleb wants instead of prediction. The goal is not to know the exact shock in advance, but to build a system that does not break badly when the shock arrives.

Fragility detection does not remove the need for planning. It changes the standard of planning from `guess the event` to `limit the damage from being wrong`.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb says the Triad was born on the spot in Korea as his answer to forecasting theater. He then gives concrete replacements: small nuclear reactors embedded deeply enough that failure does not spread, and Swedish fiscal discipline after 1991 so later crises matter less.
