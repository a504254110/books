## Core idea

The modern corporate and banking system often gives managers a convex upside while society eats the concave downside. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because it shows optionality at scale. Volatility helps the manager, hurts the public, and turns the rhetoric of `incentives` into legalized transfer of fragility. 

This ties together optionality, hidden downside, and ethics in one institutional case. Chapter 24 will extend the same problem from finance into professions, regulation, and public office. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses two linked scenes. First, stock-option managers benefit more from a jagged up-then-down market path than from a flat one, because they can cash out during the rise. Second, Robert Rubin kept about $120 million from Citibank while taxpayers absorbed the collapse.
