## Core idea

The more learning is standardized, packaged, and scripted, the less room remains for the kind of exploratory exposure that builds real-world judgment.

## Detailed explanation

Overprotection removes the very stressors and ambiguity that teach adaptation. Safety can quietly turn into domestication and fragility.

Taleb is continuing his attack on touristification, now in the educational sphere. In the whole book, this chapter's suspicion of scripts feeds directly into the critique of rationalism in chapter 17.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses the `soccer mom` as a symbol for overmanaged childhood: a parent who drives the child from one supervised activity to another and tries to remove dirt, uncertainty, and unscripted time. He contrasts that child with one who still gets biophilia, street-level messiness, and trial and error. He then adds lions in captivity, which live longer and more safely but in a thinner, degraded way.

The point of the scene: Overprotection removes the very stressors and ambiguity that teach adaptation. Safety can quietly turn into domestication and fragility.
