## Core idea

Ideas, books, movements, and reputations can gain energy from attempts to suppress or discredit them.

## Detailed explanation

These examples show that overcompensation is not only biological. In social and informational systems, an attack can become a signal of importance and trigger a stronger response than calm promotion would have produced.

Not every attack helps its target. The pattern works only when the target survives and the attack creates attention, outrage, or extra cohesion.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb gives several linked cases. Banned books by Graham Greene and Henry Miller attracted more readers because they were banned. Riots and political movements can grow when repression creates outrage and martyrs. An author can even benefit from scandal that would destroy the career of a salaried employee.
