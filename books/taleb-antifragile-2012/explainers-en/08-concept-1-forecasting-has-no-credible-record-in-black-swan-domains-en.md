## Core idea

In domains dominated by significant rare events, experts do not merely perform poorly; their long-range forecasting record is effectively nil.

## Detailed explanation

The scene shows why Taleb attacks prestige before method. A polished forecast for five years ahead means little if the same forecaster completely missed the last major shock.

Taleb needs to break the prestige of prediction before he can replace it with antifragility as the organizing lens. In the whole book, this clears space for the nonpredictive view of the world that starts in chapter 9.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb recalls a conference in Korea where Takatoshi Kato presented economic projections for 2010 through 2014. Taleb rushed to the podium and told the audience to ask for the same institution's forecasts for 2008 and 2009 made years earlier, because that is where the record would expose itself.
