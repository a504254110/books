## Core idea

Variation is sometimes a search device. A system that is allowed to wander can escape a bad local arrangement and discover a stronger one. 

## Detailed explanation

These examples show that randomness is sometimes not damage but fuel for search. Without some wandering, the system stays trapped in a bad position or unstable pattern. 

This is Taleb's positive account of disorder. He wants randomness to be seen as functional, not merely tolerable. In the whole book, the search value of variation foreshadows the optionality chapters, where trial and error beats centralized design. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb gives several linked search examples. Buridan's donkey dies if it cannot break the tie between food and water, but a random nudge can save it. In annealing, heat lets atoms move out of a stuck arrangement and cooling lets them settle into a better one. He then adds the experiment where low-intensity random shocks turn chaotic jumping balls into orderly motion.
