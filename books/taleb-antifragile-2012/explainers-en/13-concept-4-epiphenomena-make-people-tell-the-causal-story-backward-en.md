## Core idea

People often observe two things together and then infer the wrong direction of cause. That mistake becomes especially strong when institutions control the story.

## Detailed explanation

Once the direction of cause is reversed, policy and prestige both move the wrong way. People start funding, praising, or regulating the visible companion rather than the real generator.

Taleb needs this causal warning so the next chapters can attack false arrows in education, innovation, and economic history. Without it, the rest of Book IV sounds like rhetoric instead of diagnosis.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses the compass on a ship as the cleanest image: the compass reflects direction, but does not steer the vessel. He then extends this to research and wealth, and to the claim that greed newly causes every crisis, even though greed is old and the real problem is fragile system design.

The point of the scene: Once the direction of cause is reversed, policy and prestige both move the wrong way. People start funding, praising, or regulating the visible companion rather than the real generator.
