## Core idea

What looks like stabilization from outside may simply be the freezing of a fragile arrangement through external support and artificial calm.

## Detailed explanation

These cases show why Taleb distrusts imposed calm. A regime or state can look stable precisely because it is blocking small adjustments that would have released pressure earlier.

Taleb is not saying every revolt or regime collapse is good. His point is that suppressing small political variation often makes the eventual break more violent.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb points to regimes such as Saudi Arabia and pre-2011 Egypt. Outside support and repression created the appearance of order, but the tensions were only pushed underground. He also uses France and Sweden to show that the apparently successful large state is often less centralized in practice than outsiders think.
