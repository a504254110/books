# Chapter 5 · Concept 4 · English explainer

**Back to concept:** [[05-the-souk-and-the-office-building#Concept 4: Switzerland benefits from other people's shocks|Back to chapter 5 · Concept 4]]

## Core idea

Taleb is making a stronger claim than simple resilience. Switzerland is not just portrayed as surviving outside disorder. It is portrayed as gaining from it.

## Detailed explanation

This is one of Taleb's clearest political examples of `antifragility`. A robust system resists shocks and stays standing. An antifragile system is positioned so that shocks create some benefit for it.

Taleb presents Switzerland as a kind of safe haven. When other countries become unstable, frightened people, capital, and demand move toward the place that looks safer. That means outside volatility is not purely a threat to Switzerland. Under the right conditions, outside volatility sends it inflows.

The key idea is asymmetry. In calm times, Switzerland remains a functioning commercial and political system. In unstable times, it can attract even more trust, deposits, and activity. So the downside from external disorder is limited relative to the upside it may receive when other places deteriorate.

Taleb uses this to show that antifragility is not only about internal adaptation. It can also come from position. A system may benefit from where it sits in relation to the shocks hitting everyone else.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb's scenes about revolutionaries, aristocrats, wealthy depositors, and fleeing capital all point to the same mechanism. When other regimes become dangerous or unpredictable, people and money seek shelter.

The example is not saying that Switzerland is magical or that every small state becomes antifragile. It is showing a specific structure: the country occupies a position where other people's instability can redirect resources toward it.

So the real lesson is that `benefit from disorder` may come from external asymmetry, not just internal toughness. A system can become antifragile when it is arranged to receive upside from shocks that damage others.
