## Core idea

A system that needs challenge can become dull or ineffective when life becomes too smooth. 

## Detailed explanation

Both scenes show the same pattern: too little challenge weakens attention and skill, while a modest amount of resistance can sharpen performance. 

This prepares chapter 3's argument that humans should not be treated like appliances. It also supports Taleb's later preference for friction, trial and error, and environments that force adaptation. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb points to pilots whose skills dull when automation removes too much challenge; even the FAA admits that pilots can give away too much responsibility to machines. He also describes whispering during lectures instead of speaking like a TV announcer, because the audience works harder to follow and pays more attention. 

What the reader should notice in this scene is this: Both scenes show the same pattern: too little challenge weakens attention and skill, while a modest amount of resistance can sharpen performance.
