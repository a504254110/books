## Core idea

The key point in the Thales story is the asymmetry of the bet. Thales secured the right to use the olive presses cheaply and benefited disproportionately if the harvest turned out strong. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because Thales did not need to be right with precision. He only needed a deal where being right paid a lot and being wrong cost a little. 

This is Taleb's cleanest early statement that optionality beats prediction. Later chapters generalize the same structure to technology, investing, search, and career design. 

## What this example is really showing

Thales puts down deposits on olive presses around Miletus and Chios, then benefits when harvest conditions create demand.
