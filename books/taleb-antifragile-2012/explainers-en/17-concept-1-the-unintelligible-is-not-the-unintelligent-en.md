# Chapter 17 · Concept 1 · English explainer

**Back to concept:** [[17-fat-tony-debates-socrates#Concept 1: The unintelligible is not the unintelligent|Back to chapter 17 · Concept 1]]

## Core idea

A practice can be sound even if its reasons are unavailable, incomplete, or impossible to state neatly. Failure to explain is not proof of stupidity.

## Detailed explanation

Taleb is attacking a rationalist habit: treating neat verbal explanation as the only respectable form of knowledge. He thinks reality often works in the opposite order. A practice can work first, become reliable through repetition, and only later receive a partial theory.

So `I cannot explain it cleanly` and `it is unintelligent` are not the same judgment. This is a defense of tacit competence, not an attack on thinking.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb rewrites Plato's `Euthyphro` as a dialogue between Socrates and Fat Tony. In Plato's dialogue, Socrates keeps pressing Euthyphro to define `piety` exactly. Taleb imagines Fat Tony answering back: a baby does not need a definition of mother's milk to know to drink it, and a person riding a bicycle may fall if you force him to explain the theory of balance while riding.

Those scenes are not anti-theory jokes. They show that competent action can come before explicit explanation, and that demanding theory first can actually interfere with practice.
