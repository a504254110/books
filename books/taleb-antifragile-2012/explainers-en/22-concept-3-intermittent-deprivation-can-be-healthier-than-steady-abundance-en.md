## Core idea

The body can benefit from irregularity in food and comfort, not just from carefully balanced steady intake.

## Detailed explanation

It applies convexity to ordinary life. Randomness, gaps, and temporary absence can trigger adaptation in ways a steady comfortable routine does not.

Taleb is not claiming that every fast or every deprivation is good. The chapter depends on bounded stress plus recovery, not on chronic shortage.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses Greek Orthodox fasting, Lent, and the old pattern of feast-and-fast living to challenge the modern idea that every meal must be balanced and every day nutritionally smooth. He pairs this with predator versus herbivore feeding rhythms and with his attack on the breakfast-before-work habit.
