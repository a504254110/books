## Core idea

The fragilista is not simply someone who thinks. It is someone who mistakes smooth discourse, models, and office abstractions for knowledge that has survived real exposure. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because Taleb is attacking a social role, not just a bad forecast. The fragilista looks intelligent inside institutions because words and status count more there than survival under real stress. 

This chapter gives Taleb a recurring opponent for the rest of the book. The same type returns in the prediction, intervention, and policy chapters whenever protected experts mistake prestige for robustness. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb contrasts Fat Tony with a world of bankers, administrators, media people, and academic experts who produce endless talk, reports, and models. He says whole libraries of economics and social science can still miss the basic fragility that Tony sees over lunch.
