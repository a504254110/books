## Core idea

The demand for reasons often comes after practice and can misidentify what actually made the practice work.

## Detailed explanation

Taleb is not just criticizing one philosopher. He is criticizing the whole tradition that treats verbal clarity as the test of real knowledge.

Taleb is carrying forward the epiphenomena critique into philosophy itself. In the whole book, this sets up Book V, where Taleb formalizes why outcome structure matters more than narrative coherence.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb uses two scenes from Socrates' life. In `Euthyphro`, Socrates keeps demanding a strict definition of piety. In the `Apology`, he says he questioned poets and found that they could not fully explain their own poems. Taleb's answer is that this verbal pressure can misread practices that were working before the questioning began.

The point of the scene: Taleb is not just criticizing one philosopher. He is criticizing the whole tradition that treats verbal clarity as the test of real knowledge.
