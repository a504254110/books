## Core idea

In uncertain domains the cleanest way to judge belief is to inspect what a person actually risked and what actually happened, not the story he tells later.

## Detailed explanation

Postdiction has a free option built into it. A speaker can forget wrong forecasts, keep the few statements that fit the present, and look wise after the event.

Portfolio alignment does not prove that the person is right. It proves only that he is exposed enough for his opinion to count as more than cheap talk.

## What this example is really showing

Taleb's flagship case is Joseph Stiglitz and the Fannie Mae report. Before the crisis, Stiglitz and coauthors described the risk of a default by the government-backed mortgage firms as effectively zero. After the system blew up, Stiglitz wrote as if he had predicted the crisis. Taleb's rule follows directly: do not ask for the opinion first; ask what the person actually risked and what record he leaves behind.
