## Core idea

Once a person is deeply embedded in a profession, his beliefs about public matters can become self-serving without his fully noticing it. 

## Detailed explanation

This matters because it answers the chapter's main question. Taleb thinks the arrow often runs from profession to ethics, not the other way around. 

This chapter takes skin in the game from chapter 23 and applies it to ordinary professional life. It asks when personal role makes public opinion unreliable. 

## What this example is really showing

Taleb opens with the Washington tobacco lobbyist and then recalls his own first Wall Street job, where employees were told to contribute to politicians who were `good` for investment banking. In both cases, private role quietly tries to dress itself up as public principle.
