## What is grounded here

- PDF p. 5 gives the table of contents and the chapter titles for the whole book.
- PDF pp. 12-14 (`Chapter Summaries and Map`) provide short book-by-book and chapter-by-chapter framing.
- PDF p. 17 defines antifragility as distinct from resilience/robustness and lists early examples such as bacterial resistance, cities, legal systems, recipes, and the human body.
- PDF pp. 17-18 frame the project as nonpredictive decision-making under uncertainty and introduce asymmetry as a simple heuristic for spotting fragility versus antifragility.
- PDF p. 18 argues that removing volatility and stressors can weaken systems, using bed rest and muscle atrophy as an example and top-down "help" as a recurring target.
- PDF p. 19 turns the argument into an ethical rule through `skin in the game`: one actor should not gain upside while pushing downside risk onto others.
- PDF p. 20 restates the Black Swan problem and links it to nonlinear responses and modern systems that magnify unpredictability.
